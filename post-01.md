## Post Text

The generative AI skills gap is real.
But we're measuring the wrong gap.

Every organization is investing in prompt engineers, fine-tuning specialists, and AI architects. Necessary — but not sufficient.

The projects that fail don't fail because of technical execution.
They fail because no one in the room could answer:
*What does this business actually need? And is AI the right answer?*

Context and business judgment are now the scarcest resource in software teams.

The most dangerous person in an AI project is a technically excellent developer with no domain grounding.
The most valuable is someone who knows when to say: *this doesn't need a model — it needs a process.*

We are over-investing in capability and under-investing in discernment.

That gap is where projects die. And where the next generation of tech leaders will be made.
