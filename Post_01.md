The generative AI skills gap is real. But we're measuring the wrong gap.

Every organization is investing in prompt engineers, fine-tuning specialists, and AI architects. Necessary — but not sufficient. Also: expensive.

The projects that fail don’t fail because of technical execution. They fail because no one in the room could answer: What does this business actually need? And is AI the right answer?

That room includes the client. The most expensive mistake in AI projects isn’t bad execution — it’s a vendor who validated the wrong question to win the work.

How many "successful" AI projects are actually well-executed answers to the wrong question?

Context and business judgment are now the scarcest resource in software teams.

The scarce skill isn’t knowing how to use the tools. It’s knowing when the tools fit the problem — and when they don’t.

Fast adaptation isn’t adopting every new model — it’s recalibrating quickly to see if the technology serves the process, or the process serves the technology.

We are over-investing in capability and under-investing in discernment.

That gap is where projects fail — and where it becomes visible who actually understands the work.
